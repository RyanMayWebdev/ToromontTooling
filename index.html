Pre Job and Standard PPE:

Before starting any job ensure the following:

  • Are you fit and able to complete this task?
  • Have you completed a job hazard safety analysis?
  • Have you controlled all hazardous energy?
  • Are you wearing all PPE required for this task?

Standard PPE:

  • Safety glasses
  • Work Boots
  • Coveralls
  • Task specific gloves
  • Hearing protection
  • Bump cap

Additonal PPE may include:

  • Face shield
  • Respirator
  • Disposable tyvek coveralls
  • Leather gloves
  • Leather apron

Jacks

Before Use:
  • Equip proper PPE
  • Inspect jacks and pump cart for loose or missing parts, damage and leaks
  • Make sure articulation points of machine are locked
  • Have appropriate weight rated stands ready to place under machine
  • Refer to Blocking and Cribbing for information on stands and supporting machines
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Cut resist gloves
  • Hearing protection
  • Work boots
How To Use:
  • Position Jack under machine and ensure it is on a flat, even surface and that the jacking point is also flat and even. A piece of rubber or hardwood can be used to help with slightly uneven surfaces and to reduce risk of slipping
  • Connect hoses from pump cart to jack. These should be hand tight, tools should not be needed to tighten
  • Make sure wires and hoses do not create a trip hazard
  • The pump cart has a lever with 3 positions. The center position is neutral, while the left and right positions send flow to the respective port
  • Position the lever so that flow is sent to the jacks resulting in them extending, when you wish to lower them, position the lever in the opposite direction
  • Use the remote in order to power on the pump cart and slowly extend the jacks, verifying that the machine is being lifted evenly and that the jacks are staying centered and not shifting
  • If the jacks must be elevated to gain more height, support the machine and reposition the jacks. To support the machine and raise the jacks properly, please refer to proper Blocking and Cribbing techniques
  • Once you have obtained the desired height, support the machine properly, test that it is stable and then lower the jacks
  • Clean up the work area, inspect tooling after use and return to the tool crib
    • *NOTE* if you are using the hydraulic jacks with a built in pump please see the information below
  • These jacks operate similar to the ones above however they have a built in pump and don't require you to connect hoses
  • You can adjust the base height of these jacks by added spacers that are stored in the tool crib
  • Use the lever to change the direction of flow to raise or lower. Use the button on the wired controller to activate the pump

Blocking/Cribbing

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE
  • Inspect wood blocks for splitting, crush damage, grease or nails/screws
  • Stands should be free of damage, not greasy or oily and should be rated for the supported load
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Cut resist gloves
  • Hearing protection
  • Work boots
  • Kneeling pad recommended
How To Use:
  • Place wood blocks under the machine or stand/jack you wish to support
  • Blocks should be placed perpendicular to the layer below them, meaning alternate the direction of the blocks
  • The height of the cribbing should be no higher than the width of the blocks
  • Blocks should be placed towards the ends of the blocks below them, leaving a small amount of space protruding from the end.
  • Jack stands should be placed so that they evenly distribute the load, and also be on a flat, even surface
  • Wood placed on the stands will reduce the risk of slipping and provide a more level surface as they compress
  • It is a good practice that each set of stands, front or back, be able to support the entire weight of the machine

Hy-Torque

Before Use:
  • Read through the manual before use.
  • Make sure required PPE is worn
  • Inspect the tooling for any damages, leaks or missing parts
  • Connect the hy-torque s3000X to the enerpac hydraulic pump unit using the supplied hoses, hoses should be hand tight
  • Make sure the pump gauge overlay is for the s3000x, this should be marked on the overlay
  • NEVER hold the hoses or the tool itself while in use, the hoses contain 10000 psi of pressure and could severely injure you upon leaking or exploding.
    Additionally the tooling itself can jump off or pinch your hand, always keep distance between the tooling and yourself when in use.
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Oil and cut resist gloves
  • Hearing protection
  • Work boots
How To Use:
  • Once the hoses are connected, plug in the pump
  • The drive lug can be re-oriented depending on the direction you wish to turn the attached socket. This can be done by pushing in the center of the opposite side of the drive lug itself
  • Remove the drive lug and slide it in the opposite way it was removed in order to change direction, making sure the splines align
  • Ensure that the drive lug is locked in place before using the tool
  • Place your socket onto the tool and then onto the bolt to be torqued
  • Align the reaction arm against a solid surface, the arm can be realigned by pressing the latch on the side of the arm, sliding it off and then realigning. Make sure the latch is locked afterwards
  • When positioning the tool, be aware of pinch points, and also verify that the hoses will not come into contact with anything that will result in damage
  • Once the tool is positioned, position yourself in view of the pump gauge but away from the hy-torque tool itself
  • Using the remote press the on/adv button once to turn on the pump.
  • Holding the on/adv button will cause the pump to operate and allow the tool to begin rotating
  • Adjust the relief valve, by pulling up the black handle and then rotating. Before starting to torque, always back the relief valve out so the gauge reads close to 0 psi/ft lbs
  • In order to see the pressure/ ft lbs being applied, you will need to be holding the adv button. While doing this set the desired ft lbs using the relief valve handle
  • Once the desired torque has been set, hold the adv button until you see the socket rotate, let go of the button once it stops rotating. You should then here a click as the tool resets itself
  • Continue this process until the socket can no longer rotate while the gauge is reaching the set ft/lbs. The bolt should now be torqued
  • Once you are done with the tool, be sure to check for any leaks, clean up the area and put away the tooling

Press

Before Use:

Be sure to inspect press before use. Area to be inspected are:

  • Guards
  • Emergency stops
  • Cable
  • On/Off buttons
  • Latches
  • Pins are in place
Required PPE:
  • Cut resist gloves for handling parts
  • Safety glasses
  • Hearing protection
How To Use:
  • Manual can be found attached to the press
  • Ensure inspection of press has been done
  • Power on press
  • Use lever to adjust press height. Remove top pins before lifting head assembly. Remove bottom pins before lowering head assembly
  • Once all pins are installed, release tension from cable by lowering onto bottom pins
  • Adjust work plate/support so that it is centered on the work bench. Additionally supports can be found in the tool crib
  • Place your work piece onto the supports, careful to avoid pinch points
  • Center press cylinder over work piece
  • Close guarding and ensure that it is latched
  • Use remote in order to extend the cylinder until it contacts your work piece
  • Be aware that parts can explode or become projectiles when being pressed, be cautious at all times
  • Refer to the gauge on the press as well as the psi to ton chart in order to verify the pressure being applied
  • Once you have finished pressing the piece, use the remote to retract the cylinder and then power off press
  • Remove piece from work plate. Clean up and mess left behind
  • The solid flat top work plate is stored on top of the cylinder bench cage when not in use. Use the crane and magnet attachment to move

Cylinder Bench

Before Use
  • Equip proper PPE for the task
  • Refer to manual attached to cylinder bench
  • Inspect cylinder bench for any leaks, missing or broken parts and that emergency stops work
  • Check oil level of the cylinder bench as was as the nut runner planetary oil
  • Check that all levers function properly and that you know what they control
  • Ensure cylinder is clean
  • Refer to SIS documentation for the specific cylinder
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Gloves
  • Hearing protection
How To Use:
  • Use the crane to lift cylinder into place, ensure to inspect rigging and crane before use
  • Position cylinder so that the barrel is on the left side and the rod is on the right
  • Install mounting hardware into each of the pin bores on the cylinder so that the cylinder is supported
  • Ensure that the adjustable roller is supporting the end of the rod.
  • Once the cylinder is fully supported, lower the crane and move the rigging to support the end of the barrel
  • Mark the orientation of the barrel and the head
  • Remove hardware securing the cylinder head to the barrel
  • Remove any caps or plugs on the cylinder ports and slowly extend the rod end out allowing the oil to fall into the oil basin, use a deflector so that it does not spill or shoot out towards you.
  • Once the barrel and head have been removed from the barrel, drain any residual oil from the barrel and move it away from the cylinder bench drive motor
  • Find the correct socket for the nut on the end of the rod, these can be found in the cabinet next to the cylinder bench
  • Place the socket into the drive chuck of the cylinder bench and align it the the rod nut, if the red high torque cylinder is required, be sure to install both retaining pins
  • When removing or installing a nut be sure to reference the correct pressure gauge, green is for low pressure and red is for the high pressure torque cylinder
  • Refer to the psi/ft lbs chart on the wall behind the cylinder bench to ensure the proper torque is applied
  • Remove the nut and packing and reseal.
  • Before reassembly, ensure all parts are clean
  • Install the nut the same way it was removed, refer to SIS for torque specs
  • Lubricate all seals, align barrel and rod end so they are level and straight
  • Slowly move rod into barrel, once completely installed proceed with reinstalling barrel head bolts, torque to SIS spec.
  • Use crane to remove cylinder from bench, ensure that all tooling is put away and that bench is clean.

Recoil Bench

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE
  • Look up procedure on SIS, determine spring free length and adjust bench accordingly
  • Ensure recoil spring is clean
  • Inspect recoil for damage
  • Inspect recoil bench for damage, missing or loose parts. Make sure all lids close and latch properly. Check for any leaks on cylinder
  • Be aware of potential pinch points
  • Be aware that spring can explode. Spring and components may also deflect or dislodge under tension
  • Inspect crane and rigging before using to lift recoil spring
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Face shield
  • Hearing protection
  • Work boots
  • Cut resist gloves
How To Use:
  • Refer to SIS about procedure and spring free length
  • Use crane to lift recoil spring into the bench and make sure it is centered
  • Use a level if needed, align spring so that nut is accessible through the end plate
  • Before extending or retracting, make sure you are a safe distance away using the remote cable
  • Extend bench cylinder so that it gently compresses the spring, ensure it is still aligned and the detach the crane
  • Close and latch all 3 lids on the bench
  • Compress the spring slightly. Remove the nut locks and remove the nut, a 1" gun or torch may be needed
  • If the nut is seized on, see your lead hand about how to cut the spring/nut
  • Make sure the lids are all closed and then slowly retract the cylinder to the Free Length of the spring
  • Using a large prybar, verify that both ends of the spring move freely and there is no tension remaining
  • Open the lids, attach the crane and then fully retract the cylinder
  • When reassembling make sure everything is perfectly aligned and refer to SIS for reassembly
  • Make sure bench is clean after you are done and cover with the plastic cover to keep clean

Slide Sledge

Before Use:

Ensure to use the following checklist to inspect the tooling. Additionally refer to the manual for additional information

Required PPE:
  • Impact gloves with cut resistance
  • Hearing protection
  • Safety glasses
  • No loose clothing/hair
How To Use:
  • Complete checklist
  • Select appropriate sledge attachment and ensure its secured in place
  • Place one hand onto the grooved handle
  • Support the drive tube with your other hand or have another technician hold the tube
  • Place the tip of the sledge flush against the surface to be hammered
  • Ensure you are free of any pinch points
  • Pull the handle back part way out of the tube but not so far that it locks
  • Slide the handle forward with the appropriate force to loosen the hammered object
  • Ensure proper hand placement and that you are free of pinch points each successive strike

Presscart/Ram

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE
  • Inspect presscart and ram for oil level, damage, leaks and missing or loose parts
  • Make sure both ram and rod are rated for the same capacity. Thread the rod into the rod template located in the tool crib to find out the tonnage. Ask attendant for assistance if needed
  • Select the correct nuts to be threaded onto the rod, they should fit snuggly
  • Inspect nuts and rod for damaged threads, cracks, bends etc
  • Connect hoses from presscart to ram, check which direction the ram will operate before setting up on machine. Fittings should be tightened by hand
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Cut resist gloves
  • Hearing protection
  • Work boots
How To Use:
  • Always be aware of the potential for falling objects, pinch points and projectiles
  • Never stand in the line of fire, and block of the area around the work piece to prevent injury to others
  • Watch for pinch points when using presscart
  • Thread the rod into the piece you wish to remove
  • Install the ram onto the rod, if needed use the crane or another technician to assist. Be sure to inspect crane and rigging before use
  • Use spacers to fill up any gaps left on the rod, these can be found in the tool crib
  • Install the nut onto the end of the rod, the nut should be threaded on at least the full length of the nut.
  • The lever on the presscart will change the direction of the oil flow, extending or retracting the ram
  • Be sure to watch the gauge so that you don't apply too much force and exceed the rated capacity of the ram
  • Slowly begin extending the ram, listening and watching for any unusual operation
  • As you pull the work piece out with the ram, you will need to add more spacers to take up the slack

Flowmeters

Before Use:
  • NEVER hold the hoses while performing a flow test, they can get hot as well as have the potential to cause hydraulic injection injuries
  • Make sure proper PPE is worn
  • Inspect tool for damage or missing parts, check to see that hoses are in good condition
  • Refer to this manual for detailed information on each function and part
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Hearing protection
  • Oil and cut resistant gloves
  • Work boots
How To Use:
  • Before connecting hoses, relieve system pressure in the machine by keying on, enabling hydraulics and operating joysticks. Also relieve tank pressure by slowly opening the cap on the tank, SIS provides further detail on the different cap styles
  • Connect hoses to the flowmeter, the meter should be marked in and out. From the cab of the machine, the left side will be you input and the right will be the return
  • Connect the hose from the left side of the machine to the in side of the meter, and the right hose to the out side of the meter.
  • Make sure hoses are tight, you should not need to use tools to tighten the hoses
  • Connect the other end of the hoses to the machine on their respective sides. Adapters can be found on the flowmeter cart as well as the wire shelf at the rear of the tool crib
  • On the flowmeter itself, change the dial to Hi or Low depending on how much flow you expect to see, for most applications Hi is recommended
  • The black knob on the front of the meter is used to adjust the pressure, before starting a flow test, back this knob out so there is 0 pressure
  • Start the machine and operate the implement being tested, make sure the area is clear and the machine exhaust is being ventilated
  • Slowly adjust the pressure adjustment knob until the required pressure setting is displayed on the gauge
  • On the left you should see a flow rate, adjust the machine as needed until you obtain the required flow at the set psi rating.
  • Once done, be sure to relieve pressure in the machine hydraulic system before disconnecting any hoses
  • The hoses may be hot at this time, so be careful when disconnecting. Some residual oil will be in the connectors when disconnected
  • Clean up the work area, Inspect the tooling and hoses, place the hoses back on the cart and put away the tooling

Liquid Nitrogen

Before Use:

Please refer to the safety data sheet before using.

Required PPE:
  • Insulated gloves that can be easily removed
  • Safety glasses
  • Face shield when transferring to another container
  • Apron
How To Use:
  • Make sure to use in a well ventilated area
  • Select a suitable styrofoam container to transfer the liquid nitrogen to
  • Equip the required ppe above before handling
  • Bring the liquid nitrogen tank cart to your work area
  • Slowly pour a suitable amount of liquid nitrogen into the styrofoam container
  • Avoid carrying the styrofoam container once filled with nitrogen
  • Place the part into the container using tooling such as pliers or tongs
  • When placing the lid onto the styrofoam container or tank, make sure it is loosely fit
  • Use tooling to remove the part from the liquid nitrogen. *NEVER use your hands*
  • Handle the part with tooling
  • When finished slowly pour the remaining liquid nitrogen into the tank and return to tool crib

Track Press

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE
  • Inspect tool for damage, missing or loose parts and leaks
  • Larger/older pins will require more force and therefore pose a higher projectile risk
  • Be aware of falling parts as the pin is pressed out
  • Avoid pinch points when aligning the press, and never place hands around press when in use
  • Keep track press straight at all times as it may twist while under pressure
  • Make sure track tension has been released, track pads around pin have been removed and that tracks are supported
  • Block off work area to prevent personnel from entering as there is risk of projectiles
  • Verify that projectile path is clear of any objects or people, best practice would be to block it off using a bucket or other solid object
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Cut resist gloves
  • Hearing protection
  • Work Boots
  • Faceshield if needed
How To Use:
  • Connect track press to the pump cart, fittings should be hand tight, tools are not required to tighten
  • Install the appropriate size ram tip onto the track press. It should be just slightly smaller than the pin itself, verify it is completely threaded on
  • Use crane to support the track press, inspect crane and rigging prior to use
  • Position the track press c clamp portion over the pin to be removed.
  • Align the plate that matches the ram tip onto the other end of the track press, this will guide the pin
  • Ensure the tracks are supported from falling before proceeding further
  • Using the press cart, extend the ram so that tip rest up against the pin and so the plate on the other side is aligned with the opposite side of the pin
  • Check that the track press is straight and aligned with the pin, then use the press cart to continue extending the ram in order to press the pin out
  • The pin will be under a lot of force at this time and has the potential to become a projectile, be very cautious and clear the area
  • Be aware of falling parts as the pin is pushed through
  • Once the pin has been completely pressed out, retract the track press ram and use the crane to remove it from the track
  • Use the crane to lower the track
  • Inspect tool after use for any damage or leaks, clean up the work are and return tools to the tool crib

Tire Inflation

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE:
  • Do not inflate a tire if you are aware that it has been operated below 80% of its recommended inflation pressure.
    In this situation the wheel assembly must be disassembled, inspected, reassembled and inflated by a qualified tire contractor.
  • Do not apply heat to wheel studs, nuts or rims. The heat can cause explosion or a fire within the tire. See your lead hand if the lug nuts/bolts are seized
  • Support tire before removal, tires can be a crush hazard
  • Inspect tire to be deflated/inflated for damage to tire or rim
  • If there is any damage, contact a certified tire contractor
  • Support the machine
  • Position the valve stem at the top of the wheel in case it is liquid/calcium filled
  • Mark the orientation and initial pressure of the tire
  • Inspect tooling for damage, missing parts or leaks
  • Block off area in front of tire in case of explosion, there are concrete blocks for the purpose, ask your lead hand or supervisor about them
  • Refer to the manual attached to the tire inflation cart
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Hearing protection
  • Appropriate gloves
  • Work boots
How To Use:
  • Tires contain a lot of potential energy and can explode. Be sure area in front of tire is blocked off and never stand in front of the tire when inflating or deflating
  • Connect shop air to the tire inflation cart
  • Connect the tire inflation cart hoses to the tire you wish to inflate or deflate. Two tires can be connected at a time
  • Power on the unit and et the desired pressure using the + and - buttons. When deflating a tire, all tires should be deflated to between 5-10 psi to lower potential energy
  • The cart should be positioned out of the way of the tire's potential trajectory
  • Slide the blue collar into position so that air can flow to the hoses from the control unit
  • The tire will now start to inflate/deflate, when this process is complete the control unit will emit a beep to indicate it has achieved the desired psi.
  • Always verify the pressure with a manual gauge to be safe
  • If removing the tire, once the pressure is between 5-10 psi you can safely remove the lug nuts/bolts. Support the tire before removing nuts/bolts

Propane Torches

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE
  • Locate fire extinguishers in the area
  • Remove flammable materials from work space
  • Block off work area, use welding screens
  • Make sure area is well ventilated, use smoke extractor
  • Place steel plate under cutting area to prevent concrete floor from exploding due to heat
  • Inspect torches, tips for debris or damage, regulators and cylinders. Ensure that torch hoses have flash arrestors installed, see your lead hand if they do not
  • Check hoses/torch for leaks, this can be done by spraying soapy water on connections while there is pressure in the lines
Required PPE:
  • Welding glasses/mask minimum shade #3
  • Welding gloves and apron/jacket
  • Smoke extractor/respirator
  • Work boots
How To Use:
  • Open regulators on gas cylinders, propane first and then oxygen
  • Torch pressure should be set to 7-15 psi for propane and 30 to 40 psi for oxygen
  • Open the propane side of the torch slightly using the knob at the bottom of the handle
  • Use a striker to light the torch, holding the torch tip near a metal surface can help with ignition
  • Turn the propane knob until you have a flame around 6-8" in length
  • Slowly open the oxygen knob until you see the flame begin changing to blue and getting shorter
  • Adjust the knobs until the flames appear as a pointy blue triangle, you should be able to see a darker and lighter blue triangle that meet eachother
  • There are a variety of ways and applications to use a torch, see your lead hand for instruction on the correct procedure for the application
  • Be sure to never point the flame towards anyways, and to point the molten metal away from yourself and others.
  • When you have finished using the torches, be sure to shutoff the gas supply at the tanks first in order to purge the lines. Shut off in this order: Propane first then Oxygen
  • Inspect the torches and hoses for damage before putting them away, beware the tip will be hot. Check that there is still gas remaining in the cylinders, if not replace them.

Pedestal Grinder

Before Use:
  • Equip PPE
  • Do not grind wood, plastic or non iron metals on a standard wheel
  • Inspect grinder before use for any damage or loose or missing parts
  • Ensure the wheel is stopped then spin the wheel by hand to check for damages and that the wheel is balanced
  • Excessive vibration means there is an issue
  • Remove any jewelry and secure loose clothing and hair
  • Ensure guards are in place on grinder and wire wheel
  • Replace any wheel or wire wheel that is severely worn or shedding wires
Required PPE:
  • Safety glasses
  • Face shield
  • Cut resist gloves (should not be loose fitting)
  • Work boots
  • Hearing protection
  • Respirator
How To Use:
  • The green button will turn on the unit and the red will turn it off
  • When starting the grinder or wire wheel, stand off to the side, this is when the wheel is most likely to explode if damaged
  • Slowly bring work into contact with the wheel, if the work piece is small use pliers to hold it
  • Apply gradual pressure to allow wheel to heat up and to grind evenly
  • Move the piece back and forth across the wheel to prevent grooves forming in the wheel
  • Never grind on the side of the wheel
  • Allow the wheel to stop spinning before leaving
    • Wire Wheel:
  • Use pliers to hold any small work piece, slowly bring it into contact with the wire wheel
  • Apply only enough pressure to complete the task, do not use excessive force
  • If an excessive amount of wires are falling off during operation, stop operation of the wheel using the red button and replace the wheel
  • Allow wheel to stop before leaving or performing maintenance
